SUMMARY

Behub is a family-friendly app that helps to manage and organize a family’s schedule and content by collaborating among family members. I took a role in user research, informational architecture, visual design, and prototyping/Testing. For this project, I used Figma, adobe suite, Invision, Maze, and Google Forms.

PROBLEM

The busy parents with kids have an exhausting time managing a family’s schedule and support each other in the most efficient way. While they communicate via email, text, or phone call, the changes they discussed are often forgotten among other things.

SOLUTION

This collaboration platform provides clean and easy to browse calendar, folders to collect and save interesting contents, message feature to communicate and collaborate instantly, and a gentle reminder to update the schedule at the preferred time: one app to bring families and friends closer. "Hmm, finally a piece of mind.”

User Research

User Survey

The client was interested in going in the cloud storage and organization market. They had some features in mind including saving content users find on the web, organizing that content, and uploading files. The important aspect of this product was collaboration. They believed that collaboration and social sharing features were what users would like to have. Since my client did not have a clear vision for the project nor the target market, I began by user survey to test their assumptions about the user’s wants and needs.

* The survey questions were completed by over 30 people. Demographics of the responders included parents who have children, young professionals between 18-34, students, and full-time employees with a different background.

Survey Questions include

• Why did you start using the particular cloud storage or organizational app?

• Which features do you use primarily?

• When you use collaboration features, who do you often collaborate with?

• What kind of contents do you share and work collaboratively?

• What kind of contents do you think you want to share and work collaboratively if you do use collaborative features? (for those who are not current users of collaboration features)

Key Findings

1. People who currently do not use cloud storage/organizational app would like to share pictures and contents they find on the website.

2. 90% of people use collaboration features for work.

3. 40% of people use collaboration features with partner or friends.

4. 60% of parents are not satisfied with the current method of managing their family’s schedule.

Collaboration features are most often used in the work environment.

Only 40% of people use collaboration features with partners, friends or close people.

What the users are looking for

The user survey revealed that most of the respondents use cloud storage/ organizational applications for work. This led me to explore a market for parents/families who need to manage their schedules, share content, and collaborate with each other more efficiently.

Competitive Analysis

I conducted a SWOT analysis and heuristic evaluation while studying the user-flow for three competitive companies including Evernote, Pinterest, Trello.

A. Visibility of system status

B. User control and freedom

C. Consistency and standards

D. Recognition rather than recall

E. Flexibility and efficiency of use

• Instructions for use of the system for Evernote is lacking and it is difficult to remember how it works especially on the mobile app.

• Trello does not support undo and redo. It leaves users with confusion and frustration.

• The system for Pinterest caters to a wide range of users, but it lacks flexibility for experienced users.

User Persona

After the user survey and the competitive analysis, I found out that people use collaboration features for their work and there is a niche market for parents/family who can benefit from collaboration features for better communication and schedule and content management.

Based on a User survey, competitive analysis, user persona, I decided to focus on designing a family-friendly application that manages the schedule and content by collaborating among a close group of people.

The main reason why parents were not satisfied with the current method of managing their family schedule was because they tend to forget to update the calendar when changes occur. Therefore, I decided to incorporate a few different ways to make it easier for them to update their schedules painlessly. Features include the gentle reminder, the update reminder, automatic update reminder after messaging, and reminder stickers to allow users to fit their needs.

BRANDING AND IDENTITY

Branding

First, I conducted branding research, brainstorming, and mind mapping. Then I created a mood board to establish the feel and direction for this project. Since I wanted this product to be clean, calm, soothing, positive, and cheerful, I collected images, words, textures that echo those values. With the branding concept, I sketched various logo and created the final logo as well as branding and design style guide.

View Design Style Guide
Logo iterations

I sketched many variations of the logo using a bee, but they did not really click with the branding concept and vision I had for the product. After further explorations, I came up with the final logo using 3 hexagons with geometric typeface logo.

Why behub?

I chose to utilize the symbol of bee which reflects the community, brightness, and personal power and each hexagon represent each element=collaborator coming together to collaborate. The name originates from the bee, but also “being” yourself + the community, place where a user can come and collaborate together.

Typography

I used geometric typeface to convey the clean and organized look yet friendly and approachable characteristics.

color

I used neutral yet unique color to create a calming and peaceful ambiance.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

User Stories

With user research in mind, I created user stories to determine the priority of the user stories as a new user, a return user, and an administrator.

View Design Style Guide

As a New User

• I want to be able to sign up so that I do not have to retype everything.

• I want to be able to choose who I collaborate with so that I do not share the private information with anybody else.

• I want to be able to send messages in real-time so that I can coordinate the plan and add it to the calendar right away.

As a Return User

• I want to receive a reminder to add any change in schedule so that I do not forget to update them.

• I want to see the calendar so that I can add/edit my family's schedules.

User Flows

I iterated and sketched user flows by pen and paper, then I used draw.io to demonstrate the process of how users would interact with the product and the most efficient way to complete the high priority tasks.

Wire Frame Sketches

Based on the user stories, persona, and user flow, I sketched different variations of wireframes. Two key elements while designing wireframes were that all the high priority tasks needed to be visible and accessible in ease and the easy and simple navigation that could be used even for children.

USER TESTING

Usability Testing on Wire Frame

After creating the high fidelity wireframe, I wanted to test to see how users can navigate through and complete the tasks given.

Tasks

• You found an app that lets you share yours and kids schedule with everyone in the family. How would you sign up for an account?

• You are trying to add new schedule in your calendar. How can you do that?

• You found a new content in the web, you want to save it to the new group. How can you add a new group in your folder?

Based on the user feedback, I iterated a few more wireframe designs and here are 3 main improvements I made.

1. Welcome Page

Eliminated unnecessary step for users to take by creating one welcome page where users can sign up, log in, or take a tour of the main features

2. Bottom Navigation

Kept the same main bottom navigation for all the pages for consistency and smooth transition between different page

3. Remove the Dead-ends

Few screens needed to have a more obvious way for users to move on to the next screen, so I added the exit icon on the top right

A/B Testing

After I designed the high fidelity mockups, I performed A/B test for 2 particular designs which included a welcome screen for the app and welcome page instructions with illustrations or photos.

1. Welcome Page

75% of users preferred the right design.The image and the feel resonated with testers more for the family-themed app.

Main Feature Visual Design

65% of users preferred the design with the photo image instead of the illustration. They liked the clean and modern look and feel of the design.

The preference test overall helped me to see the current design objectively making it easier for me to step back and understand which design was connecting better for the target users.

Usability Testing on Prototype

I conducted the usability test to examine how my prototype was working for potential users. Overall, all the 4 tasks I instructed performed well.

• To sign up

• To add a new event on the calendar with a reminder alert

• To update the schedule and place a sticker after they are done messaging

• To add a collaborator and personalize her profile

Based on the user feedback and results, I made 3 main improvements.

1. Bolder Add Icon on the calendar main page

The add icon on the calendar main page was not standing out for some users, so I decided to change it to a bolder add icon.

2. Message Feature

During the test, 70% of users had gone to the collaborator's page to find the message feature. Therefore, I decided to move it to the collaborator page where they intuitively sought it out.

3. Logo Placement

I adjusted the background image and the logo placement to create a better visual hierarchy without interfering with each other.

The clickable usability Test after creating a prototype allowed me to study the user's real interactions and helped me to pinpoint the problems I could not see before. After I solved those issues, I believed the users can navigate the app easily and achieve what they want to do in a simple and intuitive manner.

REFLECTION

Challenges I encountered

What worked the best for me for this project was to find the right target market by conducting user research, competitive analysis, and user persona. The challenge I ran into while working on this project was the user flow of the message feature. Initially, the message feature was placed in the calendar feature. I believed that users would like to contact the collaborators when they would like to update the schedule to arrange their schedules. However, the usability testing proved that users had a difficult time finding the message feature, therefore I decided to move it to the collaborator’s page where many users were intuitively going to find it. I learned that it was crucial to get feedback from users as often as possible. It is so easy to miss something obvious when I am working on the same project for a long period of time. Future note to myself: Get the user feedback as often as possible to check the work frequently so I can offer a better solution to the real user’s problems.
